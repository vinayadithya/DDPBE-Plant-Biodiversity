Environmental and Ecological Data

1.Aegle mermelos

habitat
& Distribution:

Native to India, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, Nepal, and Myanmar.

Found in dry deciduous forests, scrublands, and riverbanks.

Grows in tropical and subtropical regions with temperatures between 5°C and 48°C.

Soil & Climate Requirements:

Thrives in well-drained sandy loam to clayey soils.

Tolerates saline and acidic soils (pH range 5–8).

Requires moderate rainfall (500–2000 mm annually) but is drought-resistant.

Prefers full sunlight but can tolerate partial shade.

Ecological Role:

Drought-resistant tree, useful in reforestation and soil conservation.

Leaves provide habitat and food for pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Fruit is eaten by birds and mammals, aiding in seed dispersal.

Acts as a carbon sink, helping in climate change mitigation.

2.Andrographis paniculata

Habitat & Distribution:

Native to India, Sri Lanka, China, Thailand, and Southeast Asia.

Found in tropical and subtropical regions, mainly in plains, hillsides, and forest edges

Grows in wild areas, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Soil & Climate Requirements:

Prefers well-drained sandy loam to clayey soils with a pH range of 5.5–7.5.

Thrives in warm and humid climates with annual rainfall of 900–1500 mm.

Grows best in temperatures between 25°C and 35°C but can tolerate up to 40°C.

Requires full sunlight but tolerates partial shade.

Ecological Role:

Fast-growing medicinal herb, used in agroforestry and intercropping.

Attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies due to its small purple flowers.

Improves soil quality by contributing organic matter.

Used in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals.
